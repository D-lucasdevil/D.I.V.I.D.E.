**File: LD-046 — “Gloom Smoking Tube”**  

**Designation: Anomalous Artifact / Psycho-Emotive Device**  
**Threat Level: Class I (Under Review — Potential Reclassification)**  
**Clearance Required: DIVIDE Level 1+**  
**Containment Status: Secured — Low Risk / Monitored**  




![redacted](https://pbs.twimg.com/media/HGtAiczasAAuSBl?format=jpg&name=large)






**Overview:**  

LD-046, designated “Gloom Smoking Tube,” is a dark blue smoking pipe decorated with intricate golden engravings. One visible marking etched into the pipe reads:  

“Property of Neo”  

And on the other side:

"let the flame eat your despair" in korean  

The artifact does not require conventional fuel, but must be manually ignited via an external spark to activate.  

**Activation Effects:**  

Upon ignition and inhalation, LD-046 produces a dense blue smoke exhibiting anomalous psycho-emotive properties.  

**Primary Effects:**  
Induces vivid recollection of negative emotional experiences  
Intensifies feelings of:  
Sadness  
Regret  
Psychological distress  
Secondary Effect (Anomalous Memory Transfer):  
Individuals exposed to the smoke may:  
Experience memories not their own  
Observe events belonging to the current user (“smoker”)  

This effect occurs even with indirect exposure, though intensity varies.  

**Post-Use Behavior:**  

At the end of a smoking session:

All nearby tear droplets begin to:  
Levitate  
Move toward LD-046  
The tears are then:  
Absorbed into the pipe  
Vaporized via a dark-blue flame  

This process occurs regardless of the subject’s resistance.  

**Observed Properties:**  
No respiratory irritation (even in non-smokers)  
No coughing or standard smoke-related effects  
Emotional impact significantly exceeds normal psychological triggers  
Testing Notes:  
**All test subjects reported:**  

Amplification of negative emotional states  
Increased likelihood of crying and emotional breakdown  
Persistent psychological discomfort after exposure  
Working Theory:   

**Proposed by [REDACTED]:**  

LD-046 utilizes human emotional output (tears) as a form of anomalous fuel source.  

**Supporting observations:**  

Increased emotional distress correlates with stronger smoke output  
Tear absorption consistently follows activation  

Status: Unconfirmed  

**Containment Protocol:**  
Stored within a reinforced, transparent containment chamber  
Ignition strictly prohibited without authorization  
Emotional exposure tests limited to approved personnel only  
Risk Assessment:  

Currently classified as low physical threat, however:  

**Psychological effects may:**  
Impair judgment  
Induce emotional instability  
Affect multiple individuals simultaneously  

Reclassification to Class II is under consideration pending long-term exposure studies.  

**Internal Memorandum:**  

“It doesn’t burn tobacco.  
It burns whatever you’re trying to forget.”
