****File: SYS-01 — “Servant Card System”****  

**Designation: Post-Termination Anomalous Artifact System**  
**Threat Level: Variable (User-Dependent)**  
**Clearance Required: DIVIDE Level 2+**  
**Containment Status: Uncontained / Monitored**  

**Overview:**  

The Servant Card System refers to a rare phenomenon in which a terminated anomaly has an extremely low probability of generating a physical artifact known as a Servant Card.  

These cards allow the holder to summon a bound version of the original anomaly.  

**Core Mechanics:**  
Acquisition  
Trigger condition: Successful termination of an anomaly  
Drop rate: Near-zero probability  
Only living / sentient anomalies are eligible  
❌ Item anomalies → no drop  
❌ Location anomalies → no drop  

***Summoning Rules***  
**Servants can be:**  
Summoned  
Dismissed  
Re-summoned at will  
If the Servant is killed once while summoned:  
The card becomes permanently inactive  
Cannot be restored or repaired  
Power Scaling:  

Servants are not exact copies of the original anomaly.  

**They are influenced by:**  

User compatibility  
Psychological alignment  
Unknown external factors  
Result:  
Some Servants are weaker than the original  
Some can become stronger under specific users  
Behavior & Control:  
Servants generally follow commands  
However, they are not fully controlled  
Known Exceptions:  
Servants may:  
Refuse commands  
Refuse to fight  
Refuse to be summoned  
**Critical Incident (SC-01):**  
A Servant rejected its master  
Killed the summoner  
Immediately de-summoned afterward  
Compatibility System:  

**When a Servant and user achieve high synchronization:**  

Reaction time improves drastically  
Combat coordination becomes near-perfect  
Power output increases  

**Recorded Case:**  
Tier I anomaly + human user  
Achieved perfect sync  
Temporarily classified as Tier III combat threat  
Limitations:  
High-tier anomalies are:  
More likely to reject control  
More unstable as Servants  
Extremely powerful Servants may:  
Ignore orders entirely  
Act independently  
Refuse participation in combat  
Trade & Black Market:  

**Servant Cards are:**  

Extremely rare  
Highly valuable  
Traded primarily within Black Street  
Market Rules:  
Cards can be:  
Sold  
Stolen (if unused)  
Once a card has been activated (summoned):  
❌ It cannot be sold  
❌ Ownership becomes bound  
Risk Level:  

**The system is classified as:**  

High Risk / High Reward  

Reasons:  

Permanent loss on death  
Unstable obedience  
Potential user termination  
DIVIDE Position:  

DIVIDE currently:  

Monitors Servant Card circulation  
Attempts acquisition when possible  
Does not fully understand origin mechanism  

Research priority: HIGH  

Internal Memorandum:  

“You don’t own them.  
You convince them not to kill you.”  

— DIVIDE Anomalous Systems Division  


***Addendum: SYS-01-A — “Card Fracture Pulse Phenomenon”***    

**Attached to: File SYS-01 — Servant Card System**  

Overview:  

Upon initial summoning of a Servant from a Servant Card, the card undergoes a micro-fracture event, accompanied by the release of an anomalous energy surge designated as a:  

“Fracture Pulse”  

This phenomenon has been confirmed in all successful summoning events.  

Fracture Behavior:  
The Servant Card develops visible cracks across its surface  
Cracks do not destroy the card, but remain permanently  
Crack patterns appear unique per anomaly  

With each subsequent summon:  

Existing fractures expand slightly  
Structural integrity of the card degrades over time  
Fracture Pulse:  

Each summoning releases a localized energy pulse, similar in nature to standard anomaly manifestation pulses, but on a reduced scale.  

Pulse Characteristics:  
Origin point: Card location  
Duration: < 1 second  
Detectable by:  
DIVIDE monitoring systems  
High-sensitivity anomaly sensors  
Pulse Intensity Scaling:  

The strength of the Fracture Pulse depends on the Servant’s effective power:  

Low-tier Servants (Class I):  
Barely detectable  
Localized disturbance  
Mid-tier Servants (Class II–III):  
Noticeable spike  
Trackable within region  
High-tier Servants (Class IV+):  
Strong anomaly signal  
Immediate DIVIDE attention triggered  
Possible temporary environmental distortion  
Operational Impact:  
Repeated summoning increases:  
Risk of detection  
Risk of card instability  
High-frequency use may:  
Attract anomalies  
Interfere with nearby anomalous systems  
Trigger unexpected secondary events  
Failure Risk (Unconfirmed):  

There are ongoing investigations into the possibility that:  

Excessive fracturing may result in:  
Uncontrolled Servant manifestation  
Permanent summoning state  
Hostile behavior deviation  

Status: Unverified  

**DIVIDE Utilization:**  

Fracture Pulses are currently used by DIVIDE to:  

Track Servant Card usage globally  
Identify unauthorized summoning events  
Locate high-value targets  
Internal Log — FP-07:  

“We thought the cards were quiet tools.  
Turns out every time you use one… you’re lighting a flare.”  

— DIVIDE Monitoring Division
