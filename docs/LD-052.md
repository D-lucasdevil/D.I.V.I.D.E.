# **File: LD-052 — “Judgement Coin”**  

**Designation:** Electromagnetic Artifact Anomaly  
**Threat Level:** **Class II**  
**Clearance Required:** DIVIDE Level 2+  
**Containment Status:** Secured — Electrical Testing Ongoing

---  

## **Overview:**  

LD-052, designated **“Judgement Coin,”** is a set of anomalous metallic coins capable of summoning highly concentrated lightning strikes upon being flipped into the air.  

As of current records:  

> **5 confirmed coins exist worldwide**  

Each coin appears visually different but shares identical anomalous properties.  

---  

## **Physical Description:**  

LD-052 resembles an aged metallic coin approximately:  

* 3.5 cm in diameter  
* Unknown alloy composition  
* Resistant to:  

  * Heat  
  * Corrosion  
  * Electromagnetic damage  

Observed engravings shift subtly during electrical activity.  

---

## **Primary Ability:**  

When LD-052 is flipped upward:  

1. Coin remains airborne longer than physically possible  
2. Atmospheric pressure drops momentarily  
3. Electrical charge accumulates above target zone  
4. A lightning strike descends within:  

   * 0.5–2 seconds  

---  

## **Targeting Behavior:**  

The lightning strike will hit:  

* The nearest conductive surface  
  OR  
* The individual who flipped the coin if:  

  * They fail to evade impact zone in time  

---  

## **Strike Characteristics:**  

Average discharge:  

* Significantly stronger than natural lightning  

Effects include:  

* Instant fatality to unprotected humans  
* Severe thermal and electrical damage  
* Localized EMP-like interference  

---  

## **Industrial Use:**  

Despite risks, DIVIDE currently utilizes LD-052 as a controlled energy-generation system.  

Procedure:  

* AT-Class personnel flip the coin beneath reinforced:  

  * Tesla absorption towers  
  * Electromagnetic grounding arrays  

Current survival rate:  

> **99%**  

Reason:  

* Lightning path is redirected before direct body contact  

---  

## **Containment Protocols:**  

All five coins are stored separately inside:  

* Vacuum-sealed nonconductive chambers  
* Deep electromagnetic isolation lockers  

Rules:  

* Never flip indoors without grounding systems  
* Never allow multiple coins to activate simultaneously  
* Unauthorized use punishable by immediate detention  

---  

## **Testing Notes:**  

### Experiment 14-B  

A coin was flipped during a thunderstorm.  

Result:  

* Atmospheric discharge increased by approximately 400%  
* Lightning strike penetrated three reinforced floors  

---  

### Experiment 22-F  

Two coins activated simultaneously.  

Result:  

* Lightning altered trajectory mid-air  
* Electrical arc formed between strikes  
* Facility blackout for 17 minutes

Further multi-coin testing suspended.  

---  

## **Threat Assessment:**  

LD-052 is classified as **Class II** due to:  

* Predictable activation  
* Relatively straightforward containment  
* High lethality only during misuse  

However:  

* Simultaneous activation potential remains poorly understood
